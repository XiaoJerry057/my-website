An ancient city that will take your breath away

Master Panorama of Bagan

Bagan is an ancient city located in the Mandalay Region of Myanmar. From the 9th to 13th centuries, the city was the capital of the Pagan Kingdom, the first kingdom that unified the regions that would later constitute modern Myanmar.

Chronicles of Bagan

SINCE 849 AD

Bagan's history dates back to early 9th century when it was founded as a small settlement. It grew to become the capital of the powerful Pagan Empire between the 11th and 13th centuries.

Key Historical Moments:

  • 849 AD: Bagan founded by King Pyinbya
  • 1044-1077: King Anawrahta unifies Burma and establishes Theravada Buddhism
  • 11th-13th centuries: Over 10,000 religious monuments built
  • 1287: Mongol invasions begin the decline of Bagan
  • 1975: Major earthquake damages many temples
  • 2019: UNESCO World Heritage Site designation

Sacred Architecture

Bagan's architectural marvels represent the finest of Burmese temple design, with influences from Mon and Pyu styles, as well as later Indian and Sri Lankan elements.

Temple Types:

  • Stupas: Solid, bell-shaped structures housing relics
  • Gu temples: Hollow temples with interior spaces for worship
  • Vaulted temples: Innovative architectural style unique to Bagan

Essential Temples

While there are over 2,000 surviving temples, these are the most significant:

Ananda Temple

Masterpiece of Bagan architecture (1105 AD)

Shwezigon Pagoda

Golden prototype for Burmese stupas

Dhammayangyi

Largest temple with mysterious passages

Thatbyinnyu

Tallest temple at 61 meters

Sulamani

Finest brickwork and murals

Htilominlo

Last major Burmese-style temple

Travel Essentials

When to Visit

The best time is between November and February when temperatures are cooler (20-30°C). March-May is extremely hot (up to 40°C). The rainy season (June-October) sees fewer tourists but some temples may be inaccessible.

Getting There

  • By Air: Flights from Yangon and Mandalay to Nyaung U Airport
  • By Bus: Overnight buses from major cities
  • By Boat: Scenic cruise from Mandalay (takes about 9 hours)

Getting Around

  • E-bikes (most popular option)
  • Bicycles (for shorter distances)
  • Horse carts (traditional experience)
  • Private cars with drivers

Preserving Bagan

Bagan faces numerous conservation challenges:

  • Earthquake damage (most recently in 2016)
  • Erosion from weather and visitors
  • Controversial restoration methods
  • Balancing tourism with preservation

As visitors, we can help by following temple etiquette: removing shoes, dressing modestly, not climbing on fragile structures, and supporting local businesses.
